Pharmaceutical drugs like Ozempic make billions of dollars every year. What are these drugs actually doing?

Ozempic is made up of GLP one agonist which simulates a hormone in our digestive system that cues satiety (feeling full). This can help some burn fat, but neglects many nutrient and metabolic imbalances that may be the root cause. Why is this important?

60% of the average American's diet is ultra processed food. What's wrong with ultra processed food?



Ultra processed food contains:

  1. Refined Sugars and High-Fructose Corn Syrup
    • Metabolic impact: Spikes blood sugar and insulin, promotes insulin resistance.
    • Associated conditions: Type 2 diabetes, obesity, NAFLD (non-alcoholic fatty liver disease), cardiovascular disease.

  2. Refined Grains and Starches
    • Metabolic impact: Rapidly digested, low satiety, contributes to blood sugar swings.
    • Associated conditions: Obesity, T2DM, cardiovascular disease.

Other properties that contribute to metabolic dysfunction include:

  • Industrial seed oils
  • Emulsifiers
  • Chemical and physical modification
  • Lack of fiber
  • Lack of nutrients, phytochemicals, and antioxidants
